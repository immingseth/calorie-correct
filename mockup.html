I've got a wedding Saturday. What should I do?

Don't bank calories all week — it usually backfires.

Eat normally Mon–Fri at your usual target. Saturday: light breakfast, normal lunch, focus on protein. Skip the morning mimosas. Then enjoy dinner and dessert.

One day above target won't move the scale. Your weekly average will. You'll be fine.

What if I want to drink at the wedding?

Pick one drink type and stick with it — wine or one cocktail.

A glass of wine is ~125 cal. A margarita is ~250. The bigger trap is reduced inhibition around food, not the alcohol calories themselves.

The whole idea, in three pieces.
Most calorie counters are only accurate if you're perfect. Most exercise apps overstate burn by 30–50%. Calorie Correct uses your weight trend as the ground truth, then calibrates everything else against it.
1
Log easy, not perfect.
Type or snap a photo of your meal. Claude parses it. Don't sweat exact portions — consistency beats precision.
2
The scale tells the truth.
Your weight trend is the ground truth. If you're not losing, your logging is off — and we'll show you by how much.
3
Calibrate weekly.
Every Sunday we update your real intake estimate, your true TDEE, and your daily target. Math, not motivation.

Design notes (for you, Seth)

Voice. "Nothing to fix, just calibrating." "You're moving in the right direction at a healthy pace." That's the brand. No exclamation points, no streaks, no "you crushed it." A doctor friend who happens to know what they're doing.

The Reality Check screen is the moat. Two lines on one chart — predicted weight from logging vs. actual weight — is the visual that no other app shows you. That image alone could be the marketing.

The "AI" pill on parsed meals is intentional. Sarah needs to feel that the magic is happening, but the rest of the app should feel like a well-designed tracker — not a chatbot in a trenchcoat.

The coach is contextual, not infinite. Best version eventually surfaces proactive suggestions ("you're going to a wedding tomorrow — want to talk it through?") rather than waiting for the user to ask.

What's missing here. Onboarding flow, weight entry screen, settings, paywall screen, dark mode. Easy to add — wanted to keep this v0.1 focused on the five screens that prove the concept.
